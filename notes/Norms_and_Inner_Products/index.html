Scaling of Price's Monotonicity Formula

Demo note showing MathJax rendering inside the new layout.

The goal of this note is to explain the relationship between inner products and norms. Along the way, we discuss some of the complexity functional analysis has over (finite-dimensional) linear algebra, and go over some concepts that break down in finite-dimensional spaces.

Definitions

Let \(V\) be a \(k\)-vector space (not necessarily finite-dimensional). A norm on \(V\) is a function \(\left\Vert \cdot \right\Vert : V \to \mathbb{R}\) such that

  1. (Subadditivity/Triangle Inequality): \[\left\Vert u + v \right\Vert \leq \left\Vert u \right\Vert + \left\Vert v \right\Vert, \text{ for all } u, v \in V.\]

  2. (Absolute Homogeneity): \[\left\Vert \lambda v \right\Vert = \left\vert \lambda \right\vert\left\Vert v \right\Vert, \text{ for all } \lambda \in k, v \in V.\]

  3. (Positive-Definiteness): For all \(v \in V\), if \[\left\Vert v \right\Vert = 0,\] then \(v = 0\).
